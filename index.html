Poverty Education Levels in the United States

Poverty levels in the United States can be broken down into groups based on how much education an individual receives. The chart above shows the poverty level percentage (per level of education) versus the population percentage (per level of education) in each state.*

This chart shows that there is a positive correlation between poverty level and population percentage for each level of education displayed. However, the overall poverty level is made up mostly of individuals with a high school education or less.

cont..

* While the chart is interactive, it displays the most useful data when the poverty level and population are selected for the same level of education.

^ The poverty data for this analysis was obtained from the 2014 American Community Survey 1-Year Estimates on factfinder.census.gov for “Poverty Status in the Past 12 Months of Individuals by Educational Attainment”.

^ The population data (per state) for this analysis was obtained from the BRFSS 2014 Overall Data from the CDCP and specifically for the question “What is the highest grade or year of school you completed?”.
